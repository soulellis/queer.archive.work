Queer.Archive.Work/riso



Queer.Archive.Work’s risograph printer is a single drum SF9450, in almost-brand-new shape. It takes almost any uncoated paper up to 11 x 17 in., from light newsprint to about 120lb. Five riso inks are available: Black, Burundy, Purple, Teal, and Fluorescent Pink (see this handy chart for reference). More colors coming soon!

Note that if you plan to do more than one pass through the machine, and/or print on both sides of a sheet, you must wait at least 24 hours before the second printing (significantly more if the air is humid). Also: this machine jams easily on large areas of ink. If your art has a lot of ink coverage, plan for more print time.

Printing on the flatbed is fairly straightforward and requires no set-up, but if you want to print directly from your Mac (from a PDF, InDesign, Illustrator file, etc.), you’ll need to install the Mac driver, available here in the studio.
